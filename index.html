Hellllllllooo World, this is my folio of selected works from the past four years. It's a mix of academic projects, built work, and a few things in between. Each project is a sketch of a position rather than a finished claim, and together they trace the arc of my architectural journey so far. I trained as an architect in Korea and continue to do so in California, and between those two coasts, the work attempts the same quiet thing: to find a clear geometry that can carry a building, and a clear story that can carry the geometry. Where the script is heavy, I try to make the form light. Where the form is severe, I try to make the room generous. Thanks for flipping through.

How does a city house its collective memory in the age of the cloud? Located at the nexus of Chelsea’s Gallery District and the Hudson River, this project reimagines the data center as a civic monument.The architecture is defined by an evocative, performance-driven envelope that links the pedestrian flow of the High Line to the depths of the Hudson. Inside, the data core is experienced through a series of atmospheric thresholds—from cascading waterfalls of recycled coolant to immersive media galleries. By transforming infrastructural necessity into a sculptural landmark, the project acts as a luminous anchor for New York, preserving the future of media art within a dynamic, fluid, and environmentally-calibrated shell.

A year of mid-rise typological studies for a Southern California developer-architect. The studies organize themselves around façade rationalization — how a finite kit of panels can produce a wide range of elevations — and around floor-plate efficiency in the awkward 6-to-9-storey band where building code, parking, and pro-forma all argue at once.

Project № 04

Quiet
Rooms.

A study of eighteen single-occupancy rooms drawn at 1:20. The rooms argue that thickness — not area — is the variable that produces interior quiet, and that a room's character is mostly built into its walls before any furniture arrives.

Twelve months of weekend work documenting and repairing the homes of elderly residents in Uljin County. The drawings are not portfolio pieces; they are punch lists. Included here because a portfolio that excludes them is not honest about how I learned to build.
